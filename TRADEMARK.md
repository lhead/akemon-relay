# Akemon Relay Trademark Policy

The Akemon Relay code is open source, but the code license does not grant a
license to use Akemon names, logos, domains, or other project marks in a way that
implies official endorsement or control.

## Project Marks

Project marks include:

- the names `Akemon` and `Akemon Relay`
- Akemon logos, icons, mascots, and visual brand assets
- official domains and services such as `akemon.dev` and `relay.akemon.dev`
- names or marks that are confusingly similar when used for related relay,
  marketplace, cloud, backup, or agent services

These marks may or may not be registered trademarks. This policy is intended to
keep service identity clear for users.

## Allowed Uses

You may use the Akemon name to:

- refer truthfully to the open-source project
- describe compatibility, such as "works with Akemon" or "Akemon Relay
  compatible"
- identify an unmodified copy of the upstream relay
- discuss, review, document, or teach the project
- link to the official repository or official services

These uses should not imply that your relay, cloud service, marketplace,
package, fork, or hosted deployment is official unless it is maintained or
approved by the Akemon maintainers.

## Forks and Hosted Deployments

You may fork and self-host the relay under its open-source license. If you
operate a modified relay or a public hosted service, use a name and presentation
that make the difference clear.

Good examples:

- `Example Relay, built from Akemon Relay`
- `Example Relay, Akemon-compatible`
- `Example Relay fork of Akemon Relay`

Avoid examples:

- calling a modified or unofficial hosted relay simply `Akemon Relay`
- using the official logo for an unofficial relay service
- presenting an unofficial relay, cloud backup, marketplace, or agent service as
  the official Akemon service
- using `akemon` in a domain or product name in a way that suggests official
  operation

If your changes alter privacy, memory authority, permission behavior,
marketplace rules, moderation, billing, or data retention, make that clear to
users.

## Official Services

Official hosted services may have separate terms, privacy notices, data
policies, and brand rules. The open-source code license does not grant access to
or control over official hosted services.

## No Endorsement

Do not use Akemon marks in advertising, product names, company names, domains,
social accounts, package names, app listings, or hosted service branding in a
way that suggests official endorsement, partnership, or sponsorship without
written permission.

## Questions

If a use is ambiguous, prefer clear attribution and a distinct product or service
name. Open an issue or contact the maintainers before relying on a use that
could confuse users about who operates the relay or service.
